# LinkedIn Outreach Playbook

The article gave 7 DM templates, all variations of one archetype (pain point → solution → low-friction CTA). This playbook expands to:

- **Connection Request** templates (≤300 chars; the article doesn't address these — but the DM templates only fire if your invite is accepted first)
- **5 DM archetypes** including the article's pain-point archetype + 4 new ones LI's similarity detector won't flag
- **12 ready-to-send DMs** across the 5 archetypes
- **T2 follow-up** + **T3 channel-switch** templates
- **Reply-handling matrix** for the 8 most common responses
- **Localized openers** in PT / ES / FR / DE / AR / JA — not Google Translate

---

## Stage 0 — The Connection Request (≤300 characters)

The article skipped this. But on LinkedIn, you can't DM someone who isn't a 1st-degree connection without paid InMail. So the invite *is* the first message.

### Hard rules for Connection Requests
1. **Always include a personal note**. Inviting without a note has acceptance rate ~5–10%. With a relevant note: 30–50%.
2. **300-character ceiling** — count, don't estimate
3. **No pitch**, no link, no question that requires more than yes/no
4. **Reference what's on their profile** — proves you're not a bulk-blaster
5. **End with an opt-out softener** — "no worries if not relevant"

### Connection Request templates

#### CR-1 — Industry-peer invite (default)
> Hi {name}, your role at {company} caught my eye — we work in the same {product_category} space, just on the supplier side ({your_country}-based manufacturing). Always learning from how distributors in {their_country} approach the market. Would love to connect.
*(~265 chars, scales easily)*

#### CR-2 — Mutual connection (when you have one)
> Hi {name}, noticed we both know {mutual_name}. I'm in {product} manufacturing on the {your_country} side; {company}'s work in {their_country} interests us. Would be great to connect — happy to share notes any time.
*(~225 chars)*

#### CR-3 — Replied to their post
> Hi {name}, your post on {topic} was the most useful thing I read this week — comment is too long for the thread, would rather connect and reply properly. We're {your_company}, manufacturing {product} from {your_city}.
*(~225 chars)*

#### CR-4 — Recent company news / award
> Hi {name}, congrats on {company}'s {recent_news} — saw it in {source}. We're {product} manufacturers on the {your_country} side; would love to connect and follow your progress.
*(~190 chars)*

#### CR-5 — Trade show / event
> Hi {name}, I think we missed each other at {event} last month. We're {your_company}, {product} manufacturing from {your_city} — would have loved to chat about your {their_segment} sourcing. Connecting now to fix that.
*(~225 chars)*

## Stage 1 — Opening DM (after acceptance)

Wait 24–48h after they accept before sending the opening DM. Sending immediately reads as templated.

### The 5 DM archetypes

1. **Pain-point + solution** (article's only archetype) — best for cold leads with no prior interaction
2. **Question for their expertise** — best for senior buyers who like being treated as authority
3. **Reverse pitch** ("we're looking for distributors in your area") — best for high-status leads who don't want to be sold
4. **Voice note opener** — highest 2026 reply rate (~3× text); best for younger buyers
5. **Insight / data drop, no ask** — best for thought-leaders / posters / decision-makers

### Archetype 1 — Pain-point + solution (article-derived, kept here as reference)

**DM-1.1: Cost stress (article Template 1, lightly edited)**
> Hi {name}, talked to a few EU/US brands recently — landed cost is the conversation everyone's having. We've done some packaging + freight optimization at our end to hold margins. If your team is hitting similar pressure, happy to compare notes. (Of course, no worries if not relevant.)

**DM-1.2: MOQ flexibility (article Template 2, lightly edited)**
> Hi {name}, quick question — is your current MOQ structure rigid, or is there room to negotiate down? We just helped a client move from 800 → 300 unit MOQ with a flexible production template. Happy to walk you through how if useful. (No follow-up if not your priority.)

**DM-1.3: Sample turnaround (article Template 3, lightly edited)**
> Hi {name}, lots of teams I've spoken to are stuck on slow sample + design feedback turnaround, especially leading into peak. Any of that hitting you? We worked out a faster workflow that I can share if it'd help.

### Archetype 2 — Question for their expertise

**DM-2.1: Genuinely asking**
> Hi {name}, you've been in the {their_segment} space for {years_at_company}+ years — wondering if you're seeing the {regional_trend} shift in {their_country}? We're trying to read where the market's heading and our {your_country} view is incomplete without local context. Even one sentence helps.

**DM-2.2: Validating a thesis**
> Hi {name}, working on a piece on how {their_country} {product} buyers are adapting to {trend} — would love your read in 1–2 lines. Will share the finished piece. No selling, promise.

### Archetype 3 — Reverse pitch

**DM-3.1: Looking for distributors / partners**
> Hi {name}, we're a {your_country} {product} manufacturer scoping for distributor partners in {their_country}. {company} keeps coming up in our research. Would there be a 15-min window in the next two weeks to see if there's overlap? If we're not the right fit no worries — happy to refer to the right contact.

**DM-3.2: Sample-set offer for evaluation**
> Hi {name}, we're shipping out a small batch of new {product} for distributor evaluation in {their_region}. We've allocated {N} sample sets. Would {company} like one? No commitment, no follow-up sequence — just feedback if you have time.

### Archetype 4 — Voice note opener

**DM-4.1 (text intro before the voice note)**
> Hi {name} — sending a 30-second voice note instead of typing this out. (Faster + you can hear my actual accent which is probably fairer than reading me write English second-language.)
> [Then send a 20–40 second voice note — see voice-note guide below]

Voice note script (record in your own voice, don't read it):
> "Hi {name}, this is {your_name} from {your_company}. We're a {product} manufacturer based in {your_city}. Saw your role at {company} — wanted to ask about something specific: {one specific question}. Send me a text reply when you have a moment, no rush. Thanks."

**Why voice notes work in 2026**:
- LinkedIn DM voice notes have ~3× text reply rate (community data, mid-2025)
- Reads as effort + authenticity, not template
- Works around language polish issues — accented spoken English is more human than over-polished written English

⚠️ Don't voice-note senior people in formal markets (JP / DE / KR senior) — feels presumptuous. Use text.

### Archetype 5 — Insight / data drop, no ask

**DM-5.1: Industry data with no CTA**
> Hi {name} — saw your post on {topic} last week. Wanted to flag one data point that pairs with what you said: {specific_stat_with_source}. No agenda, just thought it'd be useful. Happy to send the full dataset if interesting.

**DM-5.2: Spotted them at risk**
> Hi {name}, came across {issue_specific_to_their_company} (saw it on {source}). Not selling anything — just thought you might want to know if you didn't already. The {workaround/source} link is here: [link]. Hope it's useful.

⚠️ Use 5.x sparingly — it's a long-game move. Reply rate is lower (~10%) but conversion to commercial conversation 3 months later is highest among all archetypes.

## Stage 2 — Follow-up DM (T2, 5 days after Stage 1, no reply)

The article doesn't include this. ~70% of replies happen on T2, not T1.

### T2-1 (default — channel offer)
> Quick follow-up — totally OK if WhatsApp / email is where you handle this kind of conversation. Let me know which channel works (if any) and I won't keep pinging you here.

### T2-2 (give value, no ask)
> No reply needed — just wanted to share this in case it's useful: {one specific data point or article relevant to their business, 1 sentence}. Happy to send the source if interesting.

### T2-3 (humor exit)
> Following up here in case my last one got buried under {LinkedIn notification spam / their other DMs}. If now's not the right time no worries — feel free to ignore.

## Stage 3 — Channel switch (T3, 10 days after T2)

LinkedIn DM saturated. Move to email/WhatsApp using the address you got in Stage 2 enrichment.

### T3-Email opening (after LI silence)

Subject line: **`{name}, one quick question on {their_company}'s {product_category} sourcing`**

> Hi {name},
>
> Sent a couple notes on LinkedIn — assuming you're swamped, totally fair. One last attempt by email since this might be more your channel.
>
> {1-line value proposition tied to their business}
>
> Question: {one specific yes/no or short-answer question}
>
> No follow-up after this if it's not the right time — happy to circle back later.
>
> Best,
> {your_name}
> {your_company} | {your_city}
> P.S. Reply STOP and I'll remove you from any further outreach.

### T3-WhatsApp opening (if you got their number)
Use `google-whatsapp-prospecting/templates/outreach-playbook.md` first-message format. The `{source}` field becomes "your LinkedIn profile" so they understand the cross-channel link.

After T3, archive. Do not re-engage on LinkedIn for 6+ months.

## Reply-handling matrix

| Their reply | Your move |
|---|---|
| "Who are you?" | Re-introduce in 2 lines + the specific reason. Don't escalate to brochure. |
| "Send catalog" | Don't attach — describe 2 product lines inline + ask which they want details on. PDF goes only after they reply. |
| "What's your price?" | Ask their volume + destination + payment terms first. Quote second. Pre-quoting kills the conversation. |
| "Not interested" | "No problem, removed from list. Best of luck this season — happy to circle back if anything changes." Done. Don't re-DM. |
| "Send to my buyer" | Ask for the buyer's name + email + permission to mention them. "{name} suggested I reach out" doubles your acceptance rate with the new contact. |
| Voice note in their language | Reply by text, ask if EN is OK. Don't fake fluency. |
| "Currently with another supplier, happy" | "Understood — would still appreciate being on your list if you ever evaluate alternatives. Sending one short note in 6 months and that's it." |
| Ghost after acceptance | Wait 5d → T2. Wait 10d → T3 channel switch. Then archive. |

## Localized opening lines (for the first sentence of any DM)

The article uses English-only. Drop these into the opening field of any DM template above when targeting the relevant market.

### Portuguese (Brazil)
> "Olá {name}, vi seu perfil — trabalhamos no mesmo segmento de {product}, do lado da fábrica em {your_country}."

### Spanish (LatAm — informal "tú")
> "Hola {name}, vi tu perfil — estamos en el mismo espacio de {product}, en el lado de fabricación en {your_country}."

### Spanish (Spain — formal "usted" if C-level)
> "Hola {name}, le escribo porque trabajamos en el mismo sector de {product}, desde el lado de fabricación en {your_country}."

### French
> "Bonjour {name}, je vous contacte parce qu'on évolue dans le même secteur — {product} — moi côté fabrication en {your_country}."

### German (only if you've cleared compliance — see linkedin-limits.md and sister skill compliance.md)
> "Hallo {name}, melde mich, weil wir im gleichen {product}-Bereich tätig sind — von der Fertigungsseite in {your_country}."

### Arabic
> "مرحبا {name}، أتواصل معك لأننا نعمل في نفس المجال - {product} - من جانب التصنيع في {your_country}."

### Japanese (formal — keigo)
> "{name}様、突然のご連絡失礼いたします。{your_country}で{product}を製造しております{your_company}と申します。{company}様の{their_segment}における取り組みに関心がございまして、ご挨拶させていただければと思いました。"

## Tone overrides by region

| Region | Notes |
|---|---|
| 🇺🇸 USA | Skip pleasantries, lead with value. Expect quick yes/no/no-reply. |
| 🇧🇷 Brazil | "Tudo bem?" opener, informal você. Personal warmth before business. Voice notes work very well. |
| 🇲🇽 / LatAm | Tu (informal) for SMB, usted for C-level. Schedule sensitivity is loose — follow-ups fine. |
| 🇪🇸 Spain | Formal usted for cold; conversational tú after second exchange. |
| 🇩🇪 Germany | Use Sie always for cold. Avoid humor. ⚠️ Compliance hard-stop applies. |
| 🇫🇷 France | Vous always for cold. Compliments on company OK; never on personal. |
| 🇮🇳 India | Direct, formal Mr/Ms acceptable, English fine. Voice notes work. Pricing question often comes by message 2. |
| 🇦🇪 / GCC | Formal, never DM Friday/Saturday. Sunday-Wednesday peak. Voice notes OK if same gender. |
| 🇯🇵 Japan | Maximum formality, full sentences, never abbreviated. No voice notes for cold. Keigo (formal Japanese) mandatory. |
| 🇰🇷 Korea | Similar to JP — formal, structured. Use Kakao for follow-up not WhatsApp. |
| 🇨🇳 China mainland | LinkedIn shut down 2021. Use WeChat. Out of scope. |

## Volume math

If you respect LI limits + use this playbook properly:

- 80 connection requests/week → ~25–40 acceptances
- 25–40 DM-T1s → ~10–18 replies
- 5–10 of those 10–18 become real conversations
- 1–3 of those 5–10 become opportunities

For 1 deal/month: send 60–80 invites/week consistently for 3 months.

For 5 deals/month: don't try with 1 LinkedIn account. Operate 3–5 real accounts (legitimate per `linkedin-limits.md` rules), or pay Sales Navigator + double the volume.
