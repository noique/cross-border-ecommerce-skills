# Role Keywords — Localized by Country

The article gave 10 English titles and a Brazil example. Searching `"Purchasing Manager" "Brazil"` returns ≤25% of the actual Brazilian procurement population — most list their title in Portuguese.

This file localizes 50+ procurement / decision-maker titles into 8 languages and stratifies by company-size band (different titles signal different decision authority).

---

## How to choose role keywords

1. **Default**: localized title (column for the target country's primary language)
2. **Multinational target** (US/UK companies operating in your country): use English title
3. **Big enterprise (>500 employees)**: target Procurement / Sourcing tier — CEO does not sign POs
4. **SMB (10–500 employees)**: target Owner / Founder / MD — they often *are* the procurement function
5. **Very small (<10)**: target Owner directly; in many markets they're called something else (see "owner equivalents" table)

Always run **two searches**: one with localized title, one with English title. Take the union.

## Owner equivalents (small business — under 10 employees)

In many markets, "Owner" isn't the most common title — small-business owners use a trade-specific or honorific term:

| Country/region | Common owner terms |
|---|---|
| LatAm SMB | "Dueño", "Propietario", "Director" (often inflated title) |
| Brazil SMB | "Sócio", "Proprietário" |
| Italy SMB | "Titolare", "Imprenditore" |
| Spain SMB | "Autónomo" (sole trader), "Empresario" |
| GCC SMB | "صاحب المؤسسة" (owner of establishment), "مؤسس" |
| Germany SMB | "Geschäftsinhaber", "Selbstständig" |
| France SMB | "Gérant" (most common), "Auto-entrepreneur" |

## "Manufacturer-side" decision-maker titles (when you sell raw materials / components TO factories)

Different from finished-goods buyers — these people care about specs and certifications, not retail margins.

- Production Manager
- Plant Manager
- R&D Manager / Director
- Engineering Manager
- Quality Assurance Manager
- New Product Development (NPD) Manager
- Technical Director / CTO

Run a separate campaign for them with technical openers (not the article's "save costs" angle).

---

## When NOT to use a role keyword at all

If your product is small enough that a single person at the company makes all decisions:
- Target by **company size + industry** instead of role
- Open with: "Saw you run [Company Name]..." — implies you researched, no title-search needed

Examples:
- Selling custom packaging to Etsy sellers — no Etsy seller has a "Procurement Manager"
- Selling SaaS to <10-person startups — founder/CTO/anyone, role doesn't matter
